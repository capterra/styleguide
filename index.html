Stack Content

Use .stack-content within your .stack div to center and add padding to the top & bottom of your content. There are 3 stack-content options:

  • .stack-content: Use this as your base. Adds 24px padding to top & bottom of stack.
  • .stack-content-thick: Adds 48px padding to top & bottom of stack
  • .stack-content-fat: Adds 72px padding to top & 48px padding to bottom of stack
<div class="stack">
  <div class="stack-content stack-content-thick">
    Content goes here…
  </div>
</div>

Grid System

We use a 960px grid system which is composed of 12 flexible columns or "cells" with varying gutter sizes.

How it Works

  • Add a .grid to encapsulate your cells and to apply gutter space.
  • Add any amount of cells within your grid and append the width to each cell. (eg. .cell .one-sixth)

Note: Typically we try to avoid adding additional classes to the same containers as the grids and cells. For example:

<div class="grid grid-thin orange-header">
  <div class="cell one-third blue-border">
  </div>
</div>

Grid Sizes

There are 5 different grid sizes you can use to control the gutters(white space) between your cells. Use .grid as your base and append the desired grid size to it: (eg. .grid .grid-fat)

  • .grid-collapse - No gutter
  • .grid-thin - 12px gutter
  • .grid - (base) 24px gutter
  • .grid-thick - 36px gutter
  • .grid-fat - 48px gutter
  • .grid-center - Does not control the gutter, but horizontally centers the cell.

Responsive Grid

We are a desktop first site but have created classes to add to your cells for a more responsive layout. Use the .portable-*, .lap-*, and .palm-* classes in conjunction with cell widths to ensure scalability with your layout.

How it Works

  • .portable-* - Encompasses both tablet and palm devices
  • .lap-* - Tablet devices
  • .palm-* - Mobile devices
  • When adding additional device widths, ensure the order of your classes is: desktop > tablet > palm
  • You can hide the content for mobile devices using .palm-hide

Proper Formatting

    General HTML Guidelines

    • Use soft-tabs with a two space indent for nested elements and when adding multiple classes.
    • We use double quotes on attributes.
    • Paragraphs of text should always be placed in a <p> tag. Never use multiple <br> tags.
